Background

In current times of staying at home most of the day, many people are trying to find new hobbies, seek out entertainment, and engage in unique, fun activities.

When the COVID-19 pandemic forced people worldwide to stay at home, there was a complete change in lifestyle that had to take place. Suddenly people were spending more time at home than before, and many worked from home as well, only stepping out when necessary. With this development, many people started to explore new hobbies and engage in entertainment they may not have had time for otherwise. There were many who also struggled to spend time and connect with others, as the command to socially distance themselves only provided obstacles to socialization. In such a setting of potential boredom and decreased social interaction, people seek ways to fill their time with fun and make contact with other people, whether it be friends or even strangers.


The Planning Process

This may not be the first problem you think of when it comes to the everyday issues one faces, but it is no less of a reality for people worldwide.

With this idea in mind (and the fact that most of the team enjoyed playing video games in some capacity), we decided to create a video game.

Deciding on what to create, on the other hand, was more of a creative challenge than we thought. We asked ourselves, “Should we try to create something entirely new?” “Should we stick to the genres of games that people are already familiar with?” “How can we make sure that it ends up being fun?”

Then one teammate suggested a competitive variant of the game Baba is You, and the rest is history (nevermind the fact that the rest of us had never heard of it before!).

Baba is You is "a puzzle game where the rules you have to follow are present as blocks you can interact with. By manipulating them, you can change how the game works, repurpose things you find in the levels and cause surprising interactions!" (-from its official Steam page). Since this is a single-player game, we decided to put a multiplayer twist on it, which would allow people to play matches against each other.

Creating Sprites

Having taken up the title of User Interface Specialist, I was heavily involved in the graphics and appearance of the overall game. And since Gary Is Win was inspired by Baba is You, I wanted to keep the spirit of the original game’s graphics and decided to create a similar pixelated, wobbly style for our project.

And thus began the asset creation process. It took a fair amount of time, as I needed to first get familiar with Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator and then figure out how to create the different sprites and animate them. Figuring all of this out was definitely the most difficult part since I had never done anything like it before.

Building the Screen Layouts

Along with the making of the graphics, I helped to establish the high-level design of various screens, including the main gameplay screen. Together with the rest of the front end team, we constructed our designs in a collaborative project in BuilderX.


As it turned out, using BuilderX had its own share of difficulties. We were unable to work on our shared project at the same time due to limited multi-user capability, and our free trial of BuilderX would only be valid for 30 days.

Despite these obstacles, we were able to produce all the sample screens for Gary Is Win and use them as the reference point for the final product.

Conclusion

Together, the back-end and front-end teams were able to create a fun and functional game! Despite all the difficulties we faced, I’m proud of what we were able to create and learned that front-end development is something I really enjoy and want to move forward with.

Stepping out of my comfort zone and trying out new things was one of the most fun parts about this project for me. The whole experience has shown me the value of working with a team that can help each other out, and I'm looking forward to building more things that other people can enjoy.
